About Me

My name is Ishaan, and I study information science and government at Cornell University (expected graduation May 2021).  My heart belongs to statistical natural language processing, but I'm also heavily interested in machine learning (broadly) and using computational approaches to resolve phenomenological questions. In addition to my programming ventures, I serve on the City of Ithaca's Community Life Commission.

Research

Over the last year, I've been heavily involved in research at Cornell University.  My research has been conducted under Prof. Douglas Kriner and has principally focused on congressional polarization and public opinion regarding the American presidency and executive institutions.  My work during my time in the department includes the following:

  • A partisanship-predictive text classifier
  • Browser automation software to bypass the limitations of the Twitter API to aggregate historical data
  • Optical character recognition and scraping scripts for the easy collection of text from recent and historical publications
  • Topic segmentation scripts for topic area extraction in conjunction with the Comparitive Agendas Project

Community Life Commission

In January of 2019, I was appointed by Mayor Svante Myrick to serve on as a commissioner the City of Ithaca's Community Life Commission. I am set to begin my second term in January of 2020.  Here are some of the accomplishments of the Commission that I've been here to celebrate:

  • Policy analysis and legislative recommendation regarding the issue of abandoned shopping carts around the City
  • Oversight of notable public art proposals
  • The Just Play Project, an initiative to promote safe and free recreation for children 

Other Work and Side Projects
